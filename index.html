Brewing Tips

1. Use Fresh, High-Quality Coffee Beans

The foundation of a great espresso starts with the beans. Choose high-quality, freshly roasted coffee beans. Look for a roast date on the packaging and aim to use the beans within a month of that date for the best flavor.

2. Grind Your Beans Just Before Brewing

For the freshest taste, grind your coffee beans just before brewing. Use a burr grinder to achieve a consistent grind size. For espresso, you'll need a fine grind, similar to the texture of powdered sugar.

3. Measure Your Coffee and Water Precisely

The ideal espresso shot requires precision. Use a digital scale to measure 18-20 grams of coffee for a double shot. The water temperature should be between 195Â°F and 205Â°F. Use filtered water to avoid any off-flavors.

4. Preheat Your Equipment

Before brewing, ensure your espresso machine and portafilter are preheated. Run a shot of hot water through the machine to stabilize the temperature and remove any residual coffee oils from previous brews.

5. Tamp Evenly and Firmly

After dosing the coffee into the portafilter, use a tamper to press it down evenly and firmly. An even tamp ensures that water flows through the coffee evenly, extracting the best flavors.

6. Pay Attention to Extraction Time

A perfect espresso shot takes about 25-30 seconds to extract. If the shot pulls too quickly, the grind might be too coarse or the dose too low. If it pulls too slowly, the grind might be too fine or the dose too high. Adjust accordingly for the perfect shot.

7. Experiment and Adjust

Every espresso machine, grinder, and coffee bean is different. Donâ€™t be afraid to experiment with grind size, dose, and extraction time to find the perfect balance for your taste.

8. Clean Your Equipment Regularly

Regular cleaning of your espresso machine and grinder is essential to maintain the quality of your espresso. Clean the portafilter, basket, and steam wand after each use, and perform a deep clean of the machine weekly.
